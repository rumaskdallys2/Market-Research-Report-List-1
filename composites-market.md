<p><p>Composites are materials made by combining two or more distinct constituents, resulting in improved performance characteristics. Typically, composites consist of a matrix (usually a polymer, metal, or ceramic) and a reinforcement (like glass, carbon, or aramid fibers). <p><p>The composites market encompasses various manufacturing techniques, each suited for specific applications. Layup involves layering composite materials, ideal for complex shapes. Filament winding uses continuous fibers for strong, lightweight structures, commonly found in pipes and tanks. Injection molding involves injecting resin into a mold, suitable for high-volume production of intricate parts. Pultrusion creates continuous lengths of reinforced profiles by pulling fibers through a resin bath. Other methods include resin transfer molding and spray-up, expanding the versatility of composite applications across industries.</p></p>

<p><p>The medical device market features two primary types of water chillers: air-cooled and water-cooled. Air-cooled water chillers utilize ambient air to dissipate heat, making them ideal for facilities with limited water resources and requiring straightforward installation. In contrast, water-cooled water chillers rely on water from a cooling tower or other source to remove heat, offering higher efficiency and performance in larger medical settings. Both types are essential for maintaining optimal temperatures in medical devices, ensuring safety and enhancing functionality.</p></p>
